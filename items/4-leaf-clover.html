Summary

Meet "The 4-leaf Clover". As you might have guessed, luck was in my favor with this build. Why, you may ask. Well, the cherry on the cake of this build, the RTX 3060 Ti, was bought at MSRP. One of the last ones, at that. The build was for an engineering student in his first year and so there was no particular suite of application that it had to be tailored for. So, the default was the R5 3600, to nobody's surprise. It was also a very function over form type of build, and also to be kept in a dorm room, so the parts around the CPU and GPU are not flashy at all. A mid tower from antec that's pretty compact house it all. The LED strip does give it some personality while still keeping it nice and dainty. 16 gigs of ram has now become the bare minimum for any system that's not purely made for Microsoft office, so that is what you'll find here. A 240 gig ssd coupled with a 1TB hdd holds all the games and files in this one. Powering the system is a 650W psu from cooler master. A micro-ATX motherboard was no issue since it needed to be a compact system. The motherboard of choice is the MSI b450m pro-vdh max. B450 boards are such a value, considering they'll support the ryzen 5000 series, which will be the ideal platform for the next 4-5 years. I don't think people will be quick to adopt Zen 4. Especially considering the issues ddr5 is facing. Sitting atop the 3600 is a deepcool gammix gte. For the price, it works wonders. I was able to get the 3600 stable at 4.3ghz at decent temps. This is without a doubt the fastest system I've built. The 3060 ti was one of the most price efficient cards recently made. At MSRP, that is. Is MSRP even a thing anymore, though?
