Summary

This is a special build, as it's the one that started it all. Hyperbeast (as evident) was the first time I built for someone else. It's purpose was to run the suite of applications used to study and practice architecture and a great deal of gaming. At the time, in 2018, the R5 1600 seemed perfect. Although these days, it's rocking an upgraded R7 2700 picked up on a stellar deal. Paired with it, was the Gigabyte Aourus b450 Elite. A beautiful motherboard, but one with a slight bias towards orange. The RTX 2060 had just launched too, and it was a great pairing with the 1600. The 2060 was a decent little card for the price and it has held up very well, IMO, much thanks to the maturing of DLSS. I went with a modest 16 gigs of Adata's RGB memory, and a 120 gig Kingston a400 ssd along with the trusty WD blue 1tb. SSDs still very fairly expensive then. The cx550 80+ bronze rated psu from Corsair has never proven to be a bad choice, and this time was no different. Sticking with Corsair, the case of choice here was the spec-delta. A compact mid tower that came with 3 very good looking RGB fans up-front. The last piece of the puzzle was a cooler, and the ma610p was on sale for about the same price as a hyper 212 (my initial pick) and the push-pull fan configuration along with RGB really sold itself. It's doing a great job keeping in check the 2700 at 4.1Ghz, a modest overclock was never going to be an issue. The end result was a sleek, compact mid tower with rather little empty space with lots of RGB. There was nothing to complain about.
