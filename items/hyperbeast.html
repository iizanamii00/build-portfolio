Summary

White Rose 2.0 is my second iteration of a full white computer, and the third pc overall that I've built for myself. It was made to run competitive shooters, and front-end development work. Single core performance was the name of the game here. When I built it in July of 2019, I was stuck between a rock and a hard place; having to choose between the 1660 Ti and 2060. I bit bullet with the 2060 and I have zero regrets. I was impressed by the then new but sparsely implemented RTX technology, but even more so by Nvidia's DLSS. Two years down the line, I'm still able to play almost every game out there at near-ultra settings, 1080p60fps. And DLSS will probably allow me to do so for another year or so. The CPU on the other hand, was never a debate. I mostly play competitive FPS games, then CS:GO, now Valorant, and so the single core performance gains coming from a Ryzen 5 1600 were massive. The motherboard stayed. The MSI B450m mortar titanium was the only board with a white PCB, and it also performs great. The 32 gigs of RAM, I attribute to my compulsive need to fill up all 4 DIMM slots. When I saw the Adata kit on sale, I knew I had to get it. The psu was not really cherry picked; just got the very average CX 650. It gets the job done, and leaves room for upgrades. For storage, it's rocking an Intel 660p, and a couple other 250 gig 2.5" sata SSDs. The case and AIO were the most recent upgrades, and I must admit, they really do pull the whole build together. The white deepcool captain 240ex has no problems keeping the 3600 at chilly temps as you'd imagine. And the Corsair 4000D airflow is one of favourite cases; to look at as well as build in. It was remarkable easy to route and manage the plethora of cables attributing to my love for RGB. The no-hdd approach did make quite a bit of room as the hard drive tray is removable, but I'd wager the experience would be much the same regardless. The finishing touches were some cheap, yet serviceable RGB fans and a light strip. All in all, I'm extremely proud of the balance of price to performance to pretty-ness I was able to achieve with this one.
