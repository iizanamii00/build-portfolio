Summary

This is an interesting one. I call it "The traveler". Odd, as computer names go, you might think. Well that's because believe it or not, I traveled 700+ kms to build this budget gaming/workstation computer. In the middle of a pandemic, no less! And that wasn't the last of the challenges this build threw at me. It was to run Android studio and valorant, primarily. Valorant was straight forward, high ipc cores and any modern gpu would provide a great experience. As for android studio, after a bit of research, I learnt that it kind of leveraged all the threads but kind of didn't. I'm still unsure on the exact characteristics of the workload, but I figured 6 fast Zen 2 cores should pretty much do the trick. As such, I settled for the R5 3500, a chip that lives or dies by its price. At its announced price, it's one of the easiest processors to recommend. However at the time, it was a harder pill to swallow, at only a shade below the 3600. But it was the best one that fit the budget. The motherboard was a no-nonsense a320 board from msi. The GPU was truly a dilemma. The GTX 1050 Ti and the GTX 1060 3g cost about the same. However, the 3 gig frame buffer of the 1060 was definitely going to cause trouble in even moderately newer titles. Even so, I did settle on the 1060 and was able to snag the last piece in stock. It was still a great deal faster when not limited by vram and there really isn't much of a difference between medium and low textures these days. The rest was bog standard fillers. 16 gigs of corsair's trusty lpx memory, 120ssd, 1tb hdd, 450 watts bronze rated psu from antec, and also from antec, the gx202, a throwback case to say the least. However it was pleasantly easy to build in. All put together, it proved to be quite a capable machine. Greatly attributing to the R5 3500. I had no experience with the chip before, so I can't really tell if this was a golden sample, but it was an overclockers dream. With little to no hassle, I got it running a stable 4.4ghz oc at 1.23v. Safe to say, it was a successful build all things considered. Although had it been built a few months earlier, a 3600-1660 Ti machine was definitely in budget.
